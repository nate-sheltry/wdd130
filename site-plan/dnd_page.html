Summary

  Dungeons and Dragons is the most iconic tabletop game with its twenty-sided die system. Whether doing physical feats, using a skill, or attempting to attack, it is all done with the twenty-sided die. Character stats range from 1-20 and determine your capabilities in performing other tasks and what skills you are proficient with. This proficiency increases your success chances when rolling on a skill.

  What sets Dungeons and Dragons 5th Edition apart from other tabletop systems is its use of a singular bonus determined by its character level, along with streamlined conditional effects. This bonus is used for: skills, weapons, items, saving throws, spells, and other things the character is trained in. The simple way of leveling is combined with unique abilities being tied to what type of class a character is playing. Classes determine what a character can specialize in and how the characterâ€™s level progression is done. The game manages to streamline conditional effects by using a system of advantage/disadvantage where the player rolls twice. If one has advantage, they take the higher of the two rolls. If one has disadvantage, they take the lower of the two rolls.

  The simplicity of the system, the use of a singular die for ability checks, and the diversity between classes make Dungeons and Dragons 5th Edition a fun and easy game to jump into. It is this simplicity that also makes it a great game for bigger groups of players.
